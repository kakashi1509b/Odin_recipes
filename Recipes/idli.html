Idli

Description

Idli is a traditional South Indian steamed rice cake made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. It is a soft, fluffy, and spongy dish that is typically served for breakfast or as a snack. Idlis are known for their light texture and mild flavor, making them a popular choice for a healthy and nutritious meal. They are often accompanied by various chutneys and sambar, which enhance their taste. Idlis are gluten-free and easy to digest, making them suitable for people of all ages.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice and urad dal separately under cold water. Soak them in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and urad dal. In a blender or wet grinder, grind the urad dal with a little water until smooth and fluffy. Transfer it to a large bowl.
  3. Next, grind the rice along with fenugreek seeds, adding water as needed, until you get a smooth batter. Combine the rice batter with the urad dal batter in the large bowl.
  4. Add salt to the batter and mix well. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight, until it has doubled in size and has a slightly tangy aroma.
  5. After fermentation, gently stir the batter. If it's too thick, add a little water to achieve a pourable consistency.
  6. Grease the idli molds with oil or ghee. Pour the batter into the molds, filling them about 3/4 full.
  7. Steam the idlis in a steamer or idli cooker for 10-15 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Remove the idlis from the molds using a spoon.
  9. Serve the idlis hot with chutneys and sambar.
