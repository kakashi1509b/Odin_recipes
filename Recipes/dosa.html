Dosa

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. It is a thin, crispy pancake that is typically served with various chutneys and sambar. Dosa is known for its unique texture and tangy flavor, which comes from the fermentation process. It is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, and can be filled with a variety of ingredients such as potatoes, cheese, or vegetables to create different variations like masala dosa or cheese dosa.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice and urad dal separately under cold water. Soak them in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and urad dal. In a blender or wet grinder, grind the urad dal with a little water until smooth and fluffy. Transfer it to a large bowl.
  3. Next, grind the rice along with fenugreek seeds, adding water as needed, until you get a smooth batter. Combine the rice batter with the urad dal batter in the large bowl.
  4. Add salt to the batter and mix well. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight, until it has doubled in size and has a slightly tangy aroma.
  5. After fermentation, gently stir the batter. If it's too thick, add a little water to achieve a pourable consistency.
  6. Heat a non-stick skillet or tawa over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  7. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the skillet and spread it out in a circular motion to form a thin pancake.
  8. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the bottom is golden brown and crispy.
  9. Flip the dosa and cook for another minute on the other side (optional).
  10. Remove the dosa from the skillet and serve hot with chutneys and sambar.
